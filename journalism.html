journalism

Journalists keep the world sane. And if you don’t agree with that, I’d argue that journalists at least keep the world connected. I joined Carlmont’s journalism program to be close to the people who shape our communities; every article is an opportunity to showcase their stories. After serving as a staff news and opinion writer for a year, I became an editor intern and then a full-time editor. I continued reporting for our magazine The Highlander and additional stories of interest for our online publication Scot Scoop.

While editing and overseeing the writing process, I noticed points of friction that could be remedied with software. I implemented those ideas, such as the AP Style Editor, to serve our own school community as well as student journalists globally. At the end of junior year, I applied for and was granted a position on the executive board as Director of Technology and Innovation, where I continue to support student journalism. And I will, for my part, continue writing to keep the world connected too.
